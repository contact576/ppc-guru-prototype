## 1. Owner persona & emotional drivers
**[E] The owner is a clinician who became an accidental businessperson — competent and respected clinically, insecure and resentful operationally. The wound is universal: "nobody taught me to run a business," compounded by money-guilt, burnout, isolation, and an identity fused to the practice.**

**"I wear every hat / I wasn't trained for this"**
- [V] "I'm not just a physiotherapist. As a business owner, I wear so many different hats now... I'm the admin, accountant, bookkeeper, HR, marketing — I'm everything!" — JS, Emma Jack / Press Play Physiotherapy
- [V] "Many of us went to school for whatever discipline it is that we're practicing and they never taught us how to run a business. So you are literally building the plane as you're flying it." — RFD, *The Truth About Running a Small Business*
- [V] "I did not unfortunately learn how to run a business when I went to grad school." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "50% of your job is going to be wearing a business hat and 50% is the treatment... for clinic owners it's even more, it's like 80% is the business and there's so much we need to learn." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk (Ginger Desk)*
- [V] "grad school left me grossly unprepared to be a business owner, even though I was quite ready to be a therapist." — JB, John Clarke
- [V] "I don't know what I don't know... there's like so much friction at the front door to even open the front door." — RFD, *Small business financial planning*

**Identity: "this is not a business, it's just me running the show"**
- [V] "this is not a business. This is just me running the show. Two different things." — RFD, *Setting boundaries with employees*
- [V] "I didn't even call myself a business owner for a long time. I would almost downplay what I was doing, saying things like, oh, yeah, I have my own little practice." — JS, Emma Jack / Press Play
- [V] "The business is my first baby. Hands down. I still say that to my daughter. That was my first baby." — RFD, *Catch the latest with Dr. Manju*
- [V] "I didn't think I was signing up to learn these things, but I actually love it." — JS, Emma Jack / Press Play

**Money guilt**
- [V] "[clinicians are] made to feel that it's shameful to want to be profitable, because we are so accustomed to the sick-care, hospital-based model." — JS, Suzanne Jagger / Aura Aesthetics
- [V] "we undervalue ourselves so much... we're so driven to be kind and friendly and provide the best care possible, but we sacrifice ourselves before it, whether that's time or money." — RFD, *Why physical therapists feel undervalued*
- [V] "I often hear 'I don't pay myself' or 'I put everything back into the business.' So pay yourself, even if it's a small amount... I just don't think it's healthy to take nothing." — RFD, *Year-End Metrics*

- [V] "Kendall, when are you not working? Can you tell me the exact times when you do not work? And that was a turning point for me." — RFD, *How to beat practice owner burnout*

## 2. Front desk / phone / missed & after-hours calls ⭐ THE WEDGE
**[E] Owners explicitly track the phone funnel ("answer rate," "booking rate"), admit they're great at treating but bad at answering phones, churn through front-desk staff, normalize 3-day response lags, and already accept "virtual front desk" + after-hours AI booking as inevitable — but it must feel human. The product is the answer to a pain they have already named in their own words.**

**"Great at treating, not at answering phones"**
- [V] "We're not supposed to train our front desk. We've never done that job. We're great at treating patients. We're not really great at answering phones. Let's be honest." — RFD, *How to build clinic systems that scale*
- [V] "The top three to five responsibilities to focus on is how to answer a phone call." — RFD, *From onboarding to training, the first 30 days*
- [V] "It starts at the front desk — it's that phone call... a little triage to have a good match." — RFD, *Owners of Vitae Health & Sport*

**The phone IS the sale; answer-rate is a tracked KPI**
- [V] "If people are calling you and you have a good administrative team, they are your best salespeople... it's not like you're selling them on fish oil or snake oil." — RFD, *Year-End Metrics*
- [V] "I don't even know what my answer rate is, what my booking-to-prescribe rate is." — RFD, *How to build clinic systems that scale*
- [V] "There's a lot of education that happens on the phone." — RFD, *How a former NFL Career Informs a Healthcare Business* (a missed call = missed education + missed revenue)

**After-hours / weekend calls — the explicit AI opening**
- [V] "people are going to kind of learn to like that they can call on a Sunday at 2 o'clock and they're going to be able to make an appointment in the office." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully*
- [V] "We aren't going to answer the phone at 1 am, but they can absolutely visit our website to book!" — JS, Jennifer Lickver / Wellness for the Body
- [V] "those callbacks where you can very easily miss an opening, especially if it happens after the clinic has closed for the day." — JS, Julie Durnan / Ginger Desk
- [V] "nearly 20% of appointments are booked after hours or on weekends." — JB (Jane research)
- [V] "where I live at 5 o'clock it's dark now so we lock the door after 5:00, and clients know that when they arrive they can just text us or call us." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship* (after-hours contact is normal patient behaviour)

**Slow response / phone tag (the lag owners are ashamed of)**
- [V] "I'll get their autoresponder that says I'll get back to you in the next 72 hours... like oh dear, these patients are waiting 3 days sometimes for a response." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "after adding a VA we've seen an improvement in response time by 50%." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "we can relieve stress in patients by 75% just by adding a VA... patients just aren't getting what they need, there's a slower response time or questions aren't getting answered." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "Before Jane, they were literally phoning every client to try and cut down on no-show rates." — JS, Jennifer Lickver / Wellness for the Body

**Front-desk overload & staff churn (why they need it)**
- [V] "the front desk has a hard time being everything to everyone." — RFD, *Creating a virtual front desk solution*
- [V] "a really busy front desk will be just putting out fires in a reactive state... they don't actually have a chunk of devoted time." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "the staff turnover rate is just so high, and that constant pain of hiring, training, then managing a staff member only to have them quit and do that cycle all over again — it's just so expensive." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "take that load off the front desk... which can often result in losing dedicated staff due to burnout." — JS, Julie Durnan / Ginger Desk
- [V] "front desk role is not generally a long-term role for people, it tends to be pretty transitory." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully*

**The market literally asks for phone answering (demand proof)**
- [V] "there are a lot of clinics out there that want us to answer their phone... for some clinics they do just need someone to answer their phone, so we're launching that this fall." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "we hired three virtual assistants. I did one webinar to my colleagues and we launched it... we're only accepting five clients and we had this massive wait list already." — RFD, *Creating a virtual front desk solution*

**Triage scope (verbatim agent logic)**
- [V] "we do everything your front desk can do besides greeting the patients in person and cleaning the room between consults." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "if it's a simple question that might be in the treatment plan we look it up; if it's something medically specific we'll send it over to the doctor and reply on behalf of the practitioner." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "patients would call us lost on their way to the clinic." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk* (recurring logistical inbound)

**The hybrid model the wedge slots into**
- [V] "one in-house team member at the front desk for patient experience, for greeting and warmth... but they may delegate and offload a lot of the administrative duties offsite virtually, whether that be AI or a virtual company." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully*
- [V] "low touch is automated, like a reminder that their intake form hasn't been completed; high touch is picking up the phone and asking how their pain is... we can't be bespoke on every communication or we'll all burn out." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk* (defines exactly where automation belongs)

## 3. No-show / cancellation / rebooking / win-back
**[E] Retention beats acquisition (~80% of revenue is from existing patients). The richest material: rebooking-as-care, the "feels thirsty/desperate" objection to chasing patients, the no-show dunning ladder, and reactivation as a huge under-worked revenue lever (30–40% lift).**

**"80% of revenue is from existing patients / farm-the-churn"**
- [V] "for even a semi-established clinic, 80% of your revenue will come from the patients you already have or people you already know." — RFD, *Year-End Metrics*
- [V] "You need a constant stream of people that you're getting better, working to get them out of your office, and then you need to replace them." — RFD, *Get More Patients Through Referrals*
- [V] "I just care about retention." — RFD, *Year-End Metrics*

**Rebooking-as-care (the objection-dodging frame)**
- [V] "When I was working the front desk, it used to drive me up the wall when someone would come out and I'd say 'when does so-and-so want to see you again?' and they'd be like 'I don't know if I'm supposed to come back.' That person does not know the answer. They want you to say, here's when you should come back." — RFD, *Helping patients rebook as part of their care*
- [V] "Part of feeling cared for when someone's an expert is knowing what am I supposed to be doing here? What are the next steps?" — RFD, *Helping patients rebook as part of their care*
- [V] "We're booking these appointments to ensure consistency and continuity in your care, and making sure your preferred appointment times are booked for you." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully* (drop-in rebooking phrasing)
- [V] "[It] saved me from having to answer calls and emails to schedule appointments... gave me time back with my kids." — JS, Courtney Gowin / The Nest

**The "feels desperate / thirsty" objection (a core value barrier)**
- [V] "when someone cancels... that can feel like, oh no, they cancelled, I'm gonna reach out to them a hundred times, and that can feel desperate or thirsty." — RFD, *Salary vs. commission / Aphrodite Fertility*
- [V] "number two, follow-ups... a lot of people are like 'oh should I follow up, I already sent an email, now should I send another one' — I'm just like, send it, send it, send it." — RFD, *Fit Club NY*

**No-show / late-cancel policy + dunning ladder (verbatim collections language)**
- [V] "have a conversation with them after the first no-show or first late cancellation, just remind them of the policy." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "on our forms we get people to initial where it says this is a consequence for late cancellation... if there's a clause where they have to initial, they usually read that more carefully." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "there is a financial cost to missing a session... that's a spot that could have been filled by another client. Everybody's time is valuable." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "we keep credit card on file... if they've missed their session and the card doesn't work we follow up with a phone call. If they're avoiding phone calls we follow up with a letter." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship* (the dunning ladder)
- [V] "we're so happy to have you on as a client. We noticed that you missed an appointment, we tried to run the card, it was unsuccessful — wondering how you might be able to settle that." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship* (collections opener)
- [V] "to view policies as fair for everyone and not punitive... we don't want to punish you for not showing up." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "when my clients go to work they expect a full paycheck... my team work really hard, they deserve to get paid, so I have to park that guilt." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship* (money-guilt enforcing policy)
- [V] "patients just don't understand how it disrupts things on the back end, and a little communication can go a very long way." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*

**Cancellation-fill / waitlist / pivot-to-virtual**
- [V] "instead of canceling them, that appointment type would just be changed to online." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "some people have cancellation lists, so if someone needs an appointment sooner and someone cancels, they might be able to fit them in." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
- [V] "when a cancellation happens, Waitlist Notifications automatically scan the waitlist and hold the spot, so the schedule stays full without anyone having to manually call around." — JS, Do Well Therapy

**Reactivation / recall (the under-worked goldmine)**
- [V] "patient recalls / reactivations... we'll see who hasn't been in in 3, 6 or 12 months and send them emails to get them reactivated... we've seen folks increase overall revenue by 30 to 40% just by adding those reactivations." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "those who come through word-of-mouth or physician referrals stay longer in therapy than those from a Google search." — JS, Robin Valadares / Financially Fulfilled Physio

## 4. Growth / marketing / acquisition
**[E] The dominant model is "farm, not hunt" — referrals, word-of-mouth, and organic SEO/Google over paid ads. Paid ads are repeatedly described as disappointing or wasteful. Patients are found via Google + word-of-mouth; social is a verification tool. Segment buyers by capacity: "at-capacity" clinics want cancellation-fill, "needs patients" clinics want new-patient capture.**

**Farm vs hunt**
- [V] "There are two ways to get new patients: you can farm them or you can hunt for them. When we farm, we're stewarding the relationships that already exist. When we hunt, we go out to develop community partnerships." — JS, Courtney Gowin / The Nest
- [V] "focus on key referral relationships that will be faucets that never stop dripping patients out... they're always getting two or three or four patients every week referred over." — RFD, *the referral secret that fills schedules*
- [V] "it's not really Dr. Emma who's sending the person over. She's telling her front desk staff, send them to chiro. Make it easy on that person and honor that person because they often get forgotten." — RFD, *the referral secret that fills schedules*

**Referral-trust over paid ("become the obvious choice")**
- [V] "every single time I delivered an out-of-the-park client experience, word of mouth just traveled like wildfire... your patients become sort of your disciples." — RFD, *Private Practice Marketing That Actually Works*
- [V] "I became what I like to call the obvious choice. So when someone's cousin needed a therapist, they're like, I don't remember her name, but let me look up her handle." — RFD, *How to get more therapy clients through Instagram*
- [V] "the word of mouth is the biggest bang for no buck." — RFD, *Goal-setting office hours*
- [V] "My favorite referrals... I wish my whole practice were hairdressers, teachers and nurses. Those are the core three that are really good at sharing referrals." — RFD, *Building a strong online presence*

**How patients actually find clinics**
- [V] "The number one thing people say is 'I Googled it' — which is usually Google My Business or my website." — RFD, *Building a strong online presence*
- [V] "Social media is more a verification tool. They find you on Google or from word of mouth — those are the two biggest ones — then go to your social to verify it's a good choice." — RFD, *Building a strong online presence*
- [V] "every single client who comes to me finds me from Google and I've never spent a single cent on a Google ad." — RFD, *SEO for therapists*
- [V] "When someone googles The Nest, they'll see our intentional branding... start by updating your Google listing, then your website and the verbiage." — JS, Courtney Gowin / The Nest
- [V] "Turning strangers into returning clients rarely starts in a physical location anymore — it now begins online." — JB

**Ad-skepticism / paid that failed (the trust scars)**
- [V] "I have done radio marketing one time and that was crash and burn... it was 15-20 grand that did absolutely nothing — not one referral from it." — RFD, *How a former NFL Career Informs a Healthcare Business*
- [V] "I put around 20 grand into radio media and what did I see as a return? Zero, nothing." — RFD, *Goal-setting office hours*
- [V] "Facebook ads for me... they were just so low quality and it takes a lot of time to follow up with them... that just wasn't something that was beneficial for us." — RFD, *Fit Club NY*
- [V] "This company looked me in the eye and said 'your SEO will not be affected, it will be improved' — and it was not." — RFD, *Building a strong online presence*
- [V] "people skip to Google ads or Facebook ads... if you don't have the strong foundation of an SEO-optimized website with online booking, doing anything on those higher rungs doesn't make sense." — RFD, *Building a strong online presence*
- [E] **Conditional, not absolute:** a well-matched, results-proving partner is praised by name ("Duo Collective... the difference in where we saw huge new numbers of new patients" — RFD, *Aphrodite Fertility*). Distrust is about fit + proof, not paid marketing per se.

**At-capacity vs needs-patients (the segmentation signal)**
- [V] "I'm at capacity, which is fantastic, and I will never take that for granted... I can pump the brakes a little bit on selling that side on social media." — JS, Emma Jack / Press Play
- [V] "My job is 100% focused on ensuring all of my therapists are busy. That's what I care about... I cannot get paid unless everyone is busy." — JS, Jennifer Lickver / Wellness for the Body
- [V] "we'd bring on massage therapists one at a time, and once they were full we could bring on the next, so they weren't fighting over new clients." — RFD, *Owners of Vitae Health & Sport*
- [V] "My schedule is bursting at the seams as far as patient load." — RFD, *Aphrodite Fertility*
- [V] "The old adage 'build it and they will come' is no longer true." — JB, Kayla Das

**Frequency / "be findable"**
- [V] "stopping advertising is like choosing to stop a clock... McDonald's, Coca-Cola, Pepsi don't stop advertising just because they're popular." — RFD, *Park Integrative Health*
- [V] "It takes seven touchpoints for people to make a purchase. Nowadays, it's more like 17." — JB, Lisa Simone Richards
- [V] "If you yell into a megaphone in a crowd, nobody will listen. You need to speak directly to the people you want to treat." — JS, Stacey Whitcomb / Shift Acupuncture

## 5. Objections & values
**[E] Two value pillars dominate: care-over-money ("not salesy") and the human-touch authenticity guardrail. Tech hesitancy is real but is an overwhelm/unknown fear, NOT a values wall — the same owners enthusiastically welcome AI for admin/chores. The hard line: AI must sound human (not "ChatGPT"), keep a human in the loop, and be disclosable.**

**Not-salesy / care over money**
- [V] "I love the non-salesy aspect of Jane because it aligns with my own business philosophy. I feel like my work should speak for itself." — JS, Emma Jack / Press Play (the single best articulation of the tone preference)
- [V] "I'm not trying to convince them... I just say this is how it works, can you do that? I'm not trying to sell." — RFD, *Aphrodite Fertility*
- [V] "How do we really, really actually help people? After that, how do we make sure that we make money and stay alive." — JS, Jennifer Lickver / Wellness for the Body
- [V] "I want to not make decisions that are compromised clinically just to hold onto somebody... if I get desperate and worried about keeping my roster full, I worry I'll begin to make more compromised decisions." — RFD, *Tribeca Therapy*
- [V] "what's the positive for the client? They're getting their preferred times." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully* (the reframe: "salesy" → patient benefit)

**Tech / AI hesitancy (overwhelm, not values)**
- [V] "Unknown is the scariest thing. When you don't know something it's always scarier than when you do... getting over the hurdle of not knowing is the hardest part about adopting technology." — RFD, *The Hardest Part of Technology Adoption Isn't the Tech*
- [V] "Honestly, I kind of suck with technology and that sort of stuff... I was so resistant to it." — JS, Talia Singer
- [V] "we literally went from paper and pencil to Jane! I really held off for many, many years." — JS, Jennifer Lickver / Wellness for the Body
- [V] "I'm not an engineer. I'm not into this stuff generally... it was quite daunting, but you just got to do it. Just do it and then you'll be glad you did." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "it's going to move forward with or without me, and I'd rather be a part of it." — RFD, *AI in practice*

**AI welcomed for the chore / admin load (the positioning lane)**
- [V] "I love the idea of AI assisting with paperwork... we work hard and we are tired and we want to find ways to support that." — RFD, *Can ChatGPT be Your Therapist?*
- [V] "I don't want AI creating art and music for me. I want AI to help me do my taxes, the admin work... get AI to do some of your chores." — RFD, *How Clinic Owners Use AI for Social Media*
- [V] "how can this help me pick up the pieces that feel often draining as a business owner and give that energy back to my clients." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "That used to take me 25 minutes after every initial appointment. That's all gone all of a sudden." — RFD, *AI in practice*

**The "must sound human / not ChatGPT" authenticity guardrail**
- [V] "now in this day and age, flagging for ChatGPT — because our voice is authentic, our branding is authentic... let's hear some authenticity." — RFD, *Aphrodite Fertility*
- [V] "AI is not my therapist." — RFD, *Can ChatGPT be Your Therapist?*
- [V] "AI can tell you what to do, but it can't sit with you when you don't do it." — RFD, *Can ChatGPT be Your Therapist?*
- [V] "I've heard a few of these calls. It sounds a little robotic, but I think that's unfortunately the way things are going." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully* (the exact "robot embarrassing me" fear)

**Keep a human in the loop / disclose AI**
- [V] "this is just a tool. It's a draft tool. It's not a decision tool. We still have to be clinicians. We still have liability." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "that's why it's important to keep a human in the loop." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "I still have my brain that's working alongside the robot's brain and we're all just working together." — RFD, *AI in practice*
- [V] "we are human powered first and tech enabled second, and that's why we can deliver compassionate care... [investors asked] how are we going to replace this with AI — my answer was always: we're not." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "let's look for areas where we can better automate our jobs to make our jobs more enjoyable." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk* (the pragmatic framing to mirror)
- [V] "I just say, hey, I'm using this tool, it just allows me to be more present... I haven't had anyone decline. Most people say 'oh that's cool, you guys are on the leading edge.'" — RFD, *AI in practice* (the disclosure model + positive patient reaction)
- [V] "what do they do with my data... it's not being used to train the model, it's deleted after x amount of days." — RFD, *AI in practice* (the data-safety reassurance callers want)

**Standardization is OK / protect the care moment**
- [V] "think of Starbucks... you know exactly what you're going to get every time, even if it's automated, it feels good because you know what to expect." — RFD, *Rethinking the front desk*
- [V] "There is no awkward pause at the end of a session. No scrambling for a credit card. Clients can stay in an emotional, reflective place." — JS, Elana Sures / Open Space Counselling
- [V] "they don't want to spend session time completing paperwork or doing the wallet dance." — JS, Alicja Kniffen / Hands On Health

## 6. Insurance / booking / patient-experience phrasing (real words)
**[E] The verbatim front-desk and patient phrasing the agent must reproduce — first-visit greetings, rebooking lines, the direct-billing Q&A, the patient's emotional state at first contact, and the call-close.**

**First-visit / greeting (gold-standard reception script)**
- [V] "Hi Mary. Great to meet you. This must be your first time here in the office. I'm Gabrielle, I work here on the front desk. I actually booked you in the other day. Before we get you started, I'm gonna give you a little tour..." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully*
- [V] "using first name when they enter — 'Denzel, nice to meet you, 11:15, you're right on time'... a fond farewell and an invitation to return." — RFD, *What does it really take to start a practice* (the Ritz/Apple call-close model)
- [V] "We're really big on greeting people by name when they come through the door." — RFD, *Scaling thoughtfully*

**Insurance / direct-billing (front-desk decision points)**
- [V] "Are we going to direct bill? Are we going to be involved with ICBC?" — RFD, *Owners of Vitae Health & Sport* (BC auto-insurer)

- [V] "their benefits only allow them six sessions... so they come every month and a half." — RFD, *Can ChatGPT be Your Therapist?*
- [V] "most insurance companies don't cover a cost for the session you missed, so that will come out of pocket." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*

**Intake-form chase (a key booking workflow)**

**Patient emotional state at first contact (what to soothe)**
- [V] "People are coming to us often in really vulnerable places during a really hard time in their life." — RFD, *Private Practice Marketing That Actually Works*
- [V] "they finally find a practitioner they think they can trust and they book a whole bunch of sessions in... that overbooking really speaks to how much people are struggling and how relieved they are to finally find someone who can help them." — RFD, *Ending the professional relationship*
