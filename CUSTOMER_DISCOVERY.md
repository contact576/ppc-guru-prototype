*Primary validation from real practitioner owners. The only evidence that actually de-risks the bet (everything else is desk research). Log every conversation here. Honest rule: "they'd love it" ≠ "they'll pay" — only an LOI / pre-payment counts as a real yes.*

## Interviews 1–2 — Physiotherapy clinic owners (founder's network, [date: 2026-06-27])

*(Note: "Genapp"/"join app" = **Jane App** — the dominant Canadian physio EMR.)*

**What they told us:**
1. **Neither uses ANY CRM.** Both run on **Jane** only. ✅ confirms the core thesis: no marketing/sales CRM in the stack.
2. **Jane adoption is by inheritance, not choice** — they started on Jane because a past employee had used it, or it came with an acquired location. (All the founder's physios are on Jane for the same path-dependent reason.) → Jane is entrenched by *inertia*, not love → reinforces **"sit beside Jane, don't replace it."**
3. **★ Nothing is tracked before the patient enters the EMR.** Once a patient is received → into Jane → process starts. **Before that: nothing.** ✅ This is a DIRECT, unprompted confirmation of the exact wedge — the pre-patient lead layer is an open gap they *feel*.
4. **<10 staff each** — too small to have marketing ops / a CRM habit. This is the SMB segment we're targeting.
5. **They'd love it** — concretely for **tracking + automated emails for next-year insurance-renewal plans.** (Note: this is an *existing-patient retention* use case, not new-lead acquisition.)
6. **Both love the AI voice agent idea** — think it could be really important. They've received many emails from voice vendors but **never considered/acted.** Have heard of it; would like to explore.

**What this CONFIRMS:**
- ✅ No CRM / pre-EMR tracking gap is real and felt (the wedge).
- ✅ Strong appetite for **AI voice** → validates the **voice-first wedge**.
- ✅ Appetite for **email automation** (renewal reminders) → a concrete, wanted feature.

**What this RESHAPES (honest):**
- The pull is toward **AI voice + retention email** (front desk + keep-existing-patients), NOT spontaneously toward **ad→booked attribution** (our stated "moat"). For <10-staff clinics, ad-ROI may be *less* top-of-mind than voice + renewal comms. → **Lead with what they pull (voice + retention); build the ad-ROI moat behind it.** (This supports the founder's voice-first instinct.)
- "Got emails but never considered it" = **interest ≠ adoption.** Inertia is the real enemy; the founder's distribution (he already runs their ads / has the relationship) is the unlock vs. a cold vendor email.

**Caveats (don't overclaim):**
- **n = 2.** Directional only. Encouraging, not conclusive.
- **"Love it" is politeness, not purchase.** No price discussed, no LOI. The real test is willingness to pay (next calls must probe this).
- Both are the founder's own network → friendly bias.

## Interview guide — for the upcoming calls (extract decision-grade signal)

Probe past "would you like it?" into *current behavior* + *willingness to pay*:

1. **Current pre-patient process:** "When someone calls or fills a form but isn't a patient yet — what happens? Who handles it? Is anything written down?" *(map the gap)*
2. **The leak:** "Out of 10 new inquiries, how many actually book? Do you know? Do leads ever fall through?"
3. **Missed calls:** "How many calls go unanswered — especially after hours / during treatment? What happens to those people?" *(voice ROI)*
4. **Ads:** "Do you run Google/Meta ads? Do you know which ads actually bring patients? Would you want to?" *(does the ad-ROI pain land, or only voice?)*
5. **★ Willingness to pay:** "If a tool answered every call, booked the patient, and showed which ads work — what's that worth per month to you? Have you ever paid for anything like this?" *(price anchor — the key question)*
6. **Renewal use case:** "Tell me how you handle insurance-renewal outreach today." *(size the retention feature)*
7. **The inertia problem:** "You said you ignore the vendor emails you get — what would make you actually try something instead of ignoring it?"
8. **The LOI close:** "If I built this for your clinic, would you pilot it? Would you pre-commit to $X/mo if it works?" *(the only real yes)*

**Target:** 8–12 interviews. Watch for: do they volunteer the lead/ad pain unprompted (#1–4), and what number do they say at #5. If ≥3–4 say a real $/mo and would pilot → strong go.
