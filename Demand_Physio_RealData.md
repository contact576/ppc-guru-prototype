Date range 2026-03-28 → 2026-06-26 (91 days), currency CAD. This is the first of ~6 physio clinics; it anchors `/demand-scan` (Stage 2) for the physio vertical.*

Treat conversions as a directional booking signal. Demand direction is unambiguous.

## What patients ACTUALLY search (the demand language)
Spend + bookings are overwhelmingly **local-intent + city-geo** for three services: **physiotherapy, massage, RMT.**

- **"near me" / local-intent** — 628 terms · 8,507 impressions · 138.6 conv (~40% of all bookings) · ~$7.22 CPA. ("physiotherapy near me", "massage near me", "rmt near me", "physio near me")
- **physio/physiotherapy generic** — 1,277 terms · 7,030 impr · 173.2 conv
- **massage** — 779 terms · 7,515 impr · 125 conv (note: "massage near me" at 2,800 impr is the single #1 term — massage volume > physio at the very top)
- **Toronto/downtown/GTA geo** — 453 terms · 4,579 impr · 96.7 conv
- **Branded ("Bay & College")** — ~13 core terms · 41 conv · **cheapest CPA $3.41–$3.83** (brand converts best, as always)

The four generic local heads (`physiotherapy near me`, `massage near me`, `rmt near me`, `physiotherapy toronto`) alone drive ~80 conversions (~23% of bookings). Top 2 terms = 24.8% of all impressions.

## The long tail (the "curiosity, not buyers" 89%)
2,683 of 3,012 terms (89%) got impressions but **zero clicks**; 58% had a single impression. These are informational/competitor/practitioner-name queries — body parts & conditions (back, knee, sciatica, rotator cuff), modalities (shockwave, dry needling, myofascial), pediatric (49 impr, **0 bookings** — visible un-captured interest), "do I need a referral?", "cost of physiotherapy". High search variety, almost no bookings. Real buyers are the local-intent heads; the tail is research.

## Why this matters for the OPPORTUNITY (the strategic read)
1. **Demand is real, local, and phone-driven.** "Near me" searches → tap-to-call → the clinic's front desk rings. The buying journey for the money terms ends in a **phone call**, which is precisely the surface an AI front desk owns.
2. **It quantifies the AI-voice pain in dollars.** This one clinic pays **$2,315 / 91 days** to generate ~345 lead-actions at **$6.72 each**. Every one of those that rings the desk and goes **unanswered is paid-for demand thrown away.** That's the hair-on-fire pain (Stage 1) now backed by real spend — and it scales: 6 clinics × this = a measurable, recurring leak the tool plugs.
3. **Local + Canada is the demand reality** — matches the earlier competitor finding that the funded US players are US-English-centric. The demand language here is Toronto/GTA local.
