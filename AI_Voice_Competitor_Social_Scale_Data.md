## The Instagram note (important)
Instagram is **near-useless as a competitive signal here.** Proof: **Avoca, a $1B unicorn, has 256 Instagram followers.** Almost every direct competitor has **no real Instagram presence**. Only the trades-CRM giants (Jobber 95.8k, ServiceTitan 39k) and the hybrid Smith.ai (14.3k) have meaningful IG — and IG isn't where their customers are bought. **LinkedIn + employee count is the real scale signal.**

## What the data tells us strategically

1. **A massive scale chasm.** The funded tier has **15,000–330,000 LinkedIn followers and 100–3,300 employees.** The direct physio/SMB micro-competitors (Rebookly, Kickcall, AgentZap, Ainora, Greetmate, Rosie) have **2–166 followers and 0–20 employees.** No one has won the bottom tier — it's a genuine, fragmented **land-grab.**
2. **The giants are aimed elsewhere.** Enterprise CX players (Sierra/Decagon/Parloa/Cresta/Ada) are racing **up-market**; the vertical unicorns are in **home services (Avoca)** and **dental/medical (Weave/NexHealth)** — *not* physiotherapy specifically.
3. **Avoca is the cautionary tale.** It went from nothing to a **$1B home-services AI front office** — proof the model works *and* that a well-run player can take a whole vertical fast. Our planned "construction/home-services vertical 2" is effectively closed.
4. **The direct competitors are tiny and beatable on execution** — but they sit under giants who can move down-market at any time. Speed + owned distribution is everything.

## Implication
Our edge cannot be "another physio receptionist" (Rebookly/Kickcall/Ainora already there) or "home services" (Avoca owns it). It must be: **owned distribution (our 7 clinics + networks) + Canada/accent depth + reliability** — and we must move fast, because the bottom tier is an open land-grab today and the giants are circling.
