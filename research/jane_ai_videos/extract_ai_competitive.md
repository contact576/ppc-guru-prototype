## 1. What AI is Jane actually shipping / teasing?

### A. AI Scribe ("voice-to-chart") — THE product. Jane's first and only built AI feature.
Records or uploads an audio file of a patient session, transcribes it, and drafts a clinical note (SOAP / "smart templates") the practitioner reviews, edits, signs, and locks. Customizable via prompts.
- **[V]** "AICcribe is our first AI feature which was thoughtfully built for folks like you to complement the work you do in your clinics." *(V2)*
- **[V]** "the most exciting use case has been voice to chart which is the beta feature that Jane is is working on and that has been an absolute genuine Game Changer" *(V5)*
- **[V]** "once you have access to voice to chart getting started is easy simply click on the smart soap template from the new chart entry dropdown... you can either record a session right in the browser or upload an audio file before you do either make sure you get patient consent" *(V5)*
- **[V]** "AIScribe is a secure feature designed to support, not replace the clinical work." *(V2)*
- Optionality / pricing: **[V]** "it's completely optional per user per month so... only one person feels like they would benefit from voice to chart just that one person can try it" *(V5)*

### B. Smart Templates / prompt customization (a sub-feature of AI Scribe)
Practitioners write their own prompts so the note comes out in their format/voice (e.g. physio vs. mental-health). Built "generic" to span modalities.
- **[V]** "we built voice to chart in a way that's... very generic and that's why we built that smart templates feature so... we do actually have users in our beta right now that are mental health thera[pists]" *(V5)*
- Whole workshop V4 is devoted to writing these prompts — clinical only.

### C. "SEO in the Age of AI" — ADVISORY content, NOT a Jane product.
A webinar coaching clinics on how to show up in Google + AI-search (ChatGPT/AI Overviews) by strengthening their *own* digital presence: reviews, directory listings, social profiles, website content. The *only* Jane-product hook is incidental: the Jane online-booking page has high domain authority, so linking it to your site passes SEO "juice."
- **[V]** "the opportunity isn't really about picking sides... It's just about strengthening your overall online reputation and presence. So both Google and AI see you as trustworthy enough to surface you as their results." *(V3)*
- **[V]** "when you have online booking turned on and you take your logo and link it back to your website, that backlink is where all of this juice comes from. Because a japp.com website is linking to your website, it instantly gives you a boost in your SEO." *(V3)*
- Explicitly NOT positioned as a Jane marketing tool: **[V]** "It's not that Jane is better than another platform for that... it's a benefit regardless of what platform you would use." *(V3)*

### D. Using *generic* AI (ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini) for admin & marketing chores — coaching, not Jane software.
V0 and V2 teach clinics to use third-party LLMs for marketing copy, newsletters, social posts, brainstorming, and SEO suggestions. **These are not Jane features** — Jane is recommending external tools.
- **[V]** "You can also use AI to support your marketing content... drafting different kinds of marketing content, like social posts, newsletters, and blogs." *(V2 — context is ChatGPT, not a Jane feature)*
- **[V]** "if you have a website, AI can help you improve your SEO... You can do this by pasting a URL from a page on your site and asking the AI for SEO suggestions." *(V2 — generic AI)*
- **[V]** "There's so many ways to use AI um with chat GPT or CL[aude] or Claude or even Gro[c]k." *(V0)*

**Net feature list Jane SHIPS/TEASES:** AI Scribe (voice-to-chart) + Smart Templates. That's it. Everything else is education about *other people's* AI. [E]

## 3. Jane's AI POSTURE — cautious, clinical-first, human-in-the-loop, build-in-house-but-slow.

**Posture = deliberately conservative.** Jane leads with a "responsible vision," opt-in-only, no training on customer data, AI-as-suggestion-only, transparency. They are building in-house (the "JX"/AIC team) but narrowly scoped to charting.

- **Human-in-the-loop / suggestion-only:** **[V]** "any recommendations that come as an outcome of [Jane AI] is going to be merely a suggestion that you can edit, you can reject it, you can take it or leave it." *(V5)*
- **Support, not replace (clinical framing):** **[V]** "AIScribe is a secure feature designed to support, not replace the clinical work." *(V2)*
- **Opt-in, data-ownership, no-train principles:** **[V]** "when we have an AI feature an account owner will always opt into it we will never do that [on] your behalf... your data is yours and yours only we will not be using your data to train any models we're always going to be Crystal Clear when a feature is powered by AI." *(V5)*
- **Responsible-first cadence:** **[V]** "we're going to kick things off first by talking about our responsible vision for the future... what we want to really do is make sure that we're building Community confidence." *(V5)*
- **Consent-heavy:** repeated insistence on verbal + written patient consent before recording *(V5, V2)*.
- **In-house build, single team:** "Paul... senior development manager for JX, the team behind any of our AI features" *(V5)* — **[E]** one focused AI team, all output is charting.

**Read:** Jane is an **AI builder, but a slow, trust-first, clinical-only one.** They expend significant airtime on privacy/HIPAA/consent and frame AI as an assistant. This is the posture of a company protecting its EMR trust position, not one racing into adjacent growth-tech. [E]

## 4. What changes our strategy

**Strategic conclusion: the wedge is safe — for now — and Jane's own posture suggests it stays open for a while.** [E]

1. **No need to accelerate to beat Jane into acquisition/attribution.** Jane shows no lead-gen, attribution, marketing-automation, or AI-voice ambition. Their entire AI roadmap energy is clinical charting. Our pre-patient + attribution + AI front-desk wedge is uncontested by the incumbent. [E]

2. **Jane's caution is structural, not temporary.** The opt-in / no-train / consent / "suggestion-only" principles [V5] are about protecting clinical trust. An AI receptionist that *talks to prospects and books patients* is a much riskier, brand-exposing product than an internal scribe — exactly the kind of thing this cautious posture would avoid. **[E]** This is a durable reason to believe Jane won't rush our way.

3. **Jane is a partner-shaped surface, not just a rival.** They actively tell clinics to (a) turn on online booking and (b) drive their own marketing/SEO/ads — but offer no tooling to capture or attribute that demand. **[E]** Our product slots directly into that gap: we capture the lead Jane's ads/SEO advice generates and hand the booked patient to Jane's calendar. Integrating *with* Jane's booking page (the domain-authority asset they brag about, V3) is the natural seam.

4. **Watch-item, low urgency:** the only thing to monitor is whether Jane ever extends the "JX" AI team beyond charting. Today there is no signal of it. **[E]** Re-scan future "AI at Jane" releases for the words *receptionist / phone / leads / campaign / attribution / no-show / reminders-as-outreach* — none appear today.

**Bottom line:** Charting-only, cautious, in-house-but-narrow. Our wedge (lead capture · AI voice front desk · ad→patient attribution) is **entirely outside Jane's demonstrated AI scope.** Move at a deliberate pace; defend the integration seam with Jane's booking, not a footrace. [E]
