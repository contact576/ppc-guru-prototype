# Jane App — Blog Teardown (Vertical Bible + Competitive Lens)

Source: `research/jane_crawl/buckets/blog.jsonl` (217 posts). All 217 url+title skimmed; ~26 deep-read (the marketing/growth/booking/no-show/front-desk/pricing/switching/reviews cluster). Generic clinical/wellness/feature-release/newsletter/compliance posts were skipped.

Honesty tags: **[V]** verbatim/verifiable (url given) · **[M]** Jane positioning claim · **[E]** our inference.

Audience: owners of physio / chiro / massage / mental-health / allied-health / med-spa clinics (Canada + US). Voice: warm, "we ran a clinic too," community-first, anti-salesy.

## 1. Jane's positioning & messaging themes [M]

**Core identity: practice-management / EMR software, built by clinic people, sold on trust + support + ease — NOT a marketing/growth engine.** The recurring pillars across the blog:

1. **"Built by a clinic owner, for clinics."** Co-founder Ali Taylor ran a multidisciplinary clinic for 20 years; staff are ex-front-desk/clinic-managers. This is the single most-repeated trust anchor. [V https://jane.app/blog/jane-solves-your-clinic-pain] [V https://jane.app/blog/jane-vs-simplepractice-is-jane-right-for-me]
2. **Human, unlimited support as the differentiator.** "Phone wait times currently less than 1 minute"; "unlimited support six days a week by chat, phone, or email… available to everyone, on all plans. No additional fees." [V jane-solves-your-clinic-pain] [V jane-vs-simplepractice]
3. **No-lock-in / no-surprises.** "Month-to-month contracts… stay with your software because you're happy, not because you have to"; transparent pricing, no hidden fees, free data export ("Jane doesn't care to keep your data hostage"). [V jane-solves-your-clinic-pain]
4. **"Admin is expensive — give time back to care."** "You are a very expensive admin person." Software consolidates scheduling + billing + charting + documentation so clinicians do less admin. [V https://jane.app/blog/opening-a-private-practice-start-with-software-on-day-one]
5. **Cloud / security / reliability.** 99.99% uptime, 256-bit encryption "same as your banking," HIPAA + PIPEDA compliant. [V jane-vs-simplepractice] [V jane-solves-your-clinic-pain]
6. **Grow-with-you pricing.** Part-time license for <24 booked hrs/wk; flexible contracts that scale with team size. [V jane-vs-simplepractice]
7. **Community as moat.** Facebook group "janerunsclinics" (12,000+ practitioners), Allied conference, Jane University, Front Desk magazine, ambassadors. Community is a deliberate retention/brand strategy. [V jane-solves-your-clinic-pain] [V switching-emrs]

Conversion CTA on nearly every post is **"book a demo"** (jane.app/demo) — consultative, not self-serve hard-sell.

## 2. Marketing / growth posts — IS marketing in Jane's lane? [M/E]

**Verdict: Marketing is a SECONDARY, recently-expanding theme — and it is framed entirely as "you don't need an agency, use the booking/visibility tools you already have in Jane." Jane is NOT a growth/attribution platform. This is the gap.** [E, high confidence]

### What Jane SELLS as "marketing features" (its actual product surface) [V]
Three near-identical posts (therapists / med-spa / patients) define Jane's marketing offer as exactly four built-in things — none of which is advertising:
- **Jane Websites** (website builder that auto-syncs staff/services/locations from Jane; $59/mo add-on; SEO Booster add-on with keyword help). [V https://jane.app/blog/marketing-for-therapists-how-to-get-more-clients-with-jane-s-marketing-tools] [V https://jane.app/blog/how-to-market-your-practice-using-jane-s-built-in-marketing-features]
- **Online booking** ("nearly 20% of appointments are booked after hours… your booking page is a marketing surface"). [V same]
- **Reserve with Google** — adds a "Book Now" button to Google Search/Maps listing → routes to Jane booking. **"Since launching Dec 2024, over 3.6M appointments booked; nearly 500,000 first-time client bookings."** Explicitly contrasted with competitors: "Some other booking platforms charge a per-appointment fee… Jane does not." [V how-to-market…built-in-marketing-features]
- **Ratings & Reviews** — post-visit survey, optionally pushed to Google Business Profile (Thrive plan). [V https://jane.app/blog/new-in-jane-ratings-reviews]

The "tools working together" story is always the same organic funnel: **Google search → website → Reserve-with-Google "Book Now" → booking page → post-visit review → next searcher sees review.** "You didn't do anything that day except show up consistently in the right places." [V marketing-for-therapists]

### Does Jane mention ads / Google / Meta / attribution / lead source / ROI? [V — this is the key competitive finding]
- **Paid advertising is essentially absent / actively de-emphasized.** The SEO case study brags: "I went from number eight to number one in key Google searches, **and that's without running a single Google ad.**" [V https://jane.app/blog/seo-for-therapists-how-one-counsellor-used-jane-websites-and-seo-to-grow-her-business] The FAQs repeatedly say its tools are "the foundation **before any paid marketing makes sense**" / "before any outside spend is needed." [V marketing-for-therapists] [E: paid ads are framed as a later, separate, non-Jane concern.]
- **Ad tracking / attribution / cost-per-lead / ROAS: NO mentions anywhere in the read set.** [E]
- **Lead source / referral tracking: the ONLY attribution-flavored capability is a backward-looking "Referrals" report** ("diversity of referrals," "Top Patients," referral sources) — i.e., where existing patients came from, organically. Robin Valadares checks a "Referrals" report to avoid over-reliance on one doctor. This is referral-mix analytics, NOT marketing-channel/ad attribution. [V https://jane.app/blog/using-clinic-data-to-make-better-business-decisions]
- **ROI is discussed only as clinic financial literacy** (revenue vs expenses, "return on time," surplus/deficit, retention %) — never as marketing ROI/CAC. [V using-clinic-data]
- When the blog DOES teach real demand-gen (paid social, influencers, UGC, radio/TV/PR, awards, Meta/TikTok/IG, freelancers via Fiverr/Upwork/Toptal), it is **always third-party guest content** (PR strategist Lisa Simone Richards, coach John Clarke, coach Kayla Das, Dr. Rebecca Hopkins) pointing OUT to non-Jane tools/agencies — not Jane features. [V https://jane.app/blog/visibility-101-the-power-of-health-and-wellness-marketing] [V https://jane.app/blog/marketing-for-wellness-4-ways-to-attract-new-clients] [V https://jane.app/blog/helpful-tips-to-start-your-clinic-s-social-media-presence]

**Implication for PPC Guru / our teardown [E]:** Jane owns the *bottom of the funnel* (capture: booking page, Reserve-with-Google, reviews, website-as-conversion-surface) and explicitly frames itself as "you won't need an agency." It does **not** own the *top of the funnel* (paid acquisition) and has **zero** ad-attribution, lead-source-to-ad, spend, or ROAS capability. A clinic running Meta/Google ads gets no help from Jane connecting ad spend → booked patient → revenue. That measurement/attribution layer is a clean white space Jane has chosen not to enter (and even argues against needing). The flip risk: Reserve-with-Google + free organic booking is Jane's "you don't need to pay for acquisition" wedge — our pitch must show paid acquisition + attribution as additive on top of it, not competitive with it.

## 5. Sharpest competitive takeaways [E]

- **Jane = booking + capture + practice-ops + trust/support/community.** It deliberately positions as the anti-agency: "you probably don't need a marketing agency or a separate subscription." Strong moat at bottom-of-funnel conversion (Reserve with Google is a real, large, free distribution asset: 3.6M bookings / 500k first-timers).
- **No paid-acquisition, no ad-attribution, no lead-source-to-spend-to-revenue tracking anywhere.** Jane explicitly frames paid ads as "later / not needed yet." That measurement layer (what an agency like PPC Guru's client runs on Meta/Google) is entirely outside Jane — a genuine white space, but also a wedge Jane uses to tell owners they can skip paid acquisition.
- **The agency angle vs Jane:** an ad agency must show its work in terms Jane never provides — cost per booked patient, channel ROI, ad → Jane-booking attribution. Integrating with / sitting on top of Jane's booking + Reserve-with-Google funnel (rather than replacing it) is the natural play.
