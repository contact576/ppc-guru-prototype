# Radio Front Desk — Bundle 5 Extract (16 episodes)

Source: `research/radio_front_desk/bundles/bundle_5.md`
Vertical: allied-health clinics (physio/chiro/RMT/massage/naturopathic/mental-health), mostly Canada.
Purpose: (a) Vertical Bible of clinic-owner / front-desk / patient language; (b) AI voice-agent script for missed / after-hours clinic calls.
Honesty tags: `[V]` verbatim (episode cited), `[E]` synthesis. ASR errors flagged `[sic]`.

Episodes read: 4, 9, 14, 19, 24, 29, 34, 39, 44, 49, 54, 59, 64, 69, 74, 79.
NOTE: Two episodes are gold for the voice-agent wedge — **Ep 69 ("Creating a virtual front desk solution")** and **Ep 74 ("Rethinking the front desk… Ginger Desk")** — both are about replacing/augmenting the clinic front desk with virtual assistants. Ep 79 is the deepest source on no-shows / late-cancels / policy language.

## 2. FRONT DESK / PHONE / MISSED-CALL / NO-SHOW PAIN  ← highest priority

### Front-desk overload & "everything to everyone"
- `[V]` "the front desk has a hard time being everything to everyone when they're originally hired from one discipline so let's say an rmt hires them well the physios may not get the exact support they need." — *Ep 69, Creating a virtual front desk solution*
- `[V]` "a really busy front desk will be just putting out fires in a reactive state… they don't actually have like a chunk of devoted time." — *Ep 74, Rethinking the front desk*
- `[V]` "the staff turnover rate is just so high and that constant pain of hiring training and then managing a staff member only to have them quit and have to do that cycle all over again it's just so expensive." — *Ep 74* (the #1 reason owners hire Ginger Desk)
- `[V]` "they were sending emails between consults, some of them were sending out invoices after hours or returning phone calls… it sounded like she was doing about 50% of the [admin] on her own." — *Ep 74* (the owner/practitioner is doing front-desk work themselves)

### Slow response time / phone-tag (the voice-agent wedge)
- `[V]` "I'll get their autoresponder that says I'll get back to you in the next 72 hours… like oh dear these patients are waiting 3 days sometimes for a response." — *Ep 74, Rethinking the front desk*
- `[V]` "when we've added a VA on average in clinics we've seen that there's been an improvement in response time by 50%." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "we can relieve stress in patients by 75% just by adding a VA to the clinic… patients just aren't getting what they need… there's a slower response time or maybe questions aren't getting answered or they find it hard to get in off the wait list." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "patients would call us lost on their way to the clinic" — recurring inbound calls that a system can pre-empt. — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "there are a lot of clinics out there that want us to answer their phone… for some clinics they do just need someone to answer their phone so we're launching that this fall [live phone answering]." — *Ep 74* (validates the missed-call answering market directly)

### What a virtual/AI front desk actually does (scope for the agent)
- `[V]` "we do everything your front desk can do besides greeting the patients in person and cleaning the room between consults." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "if it's a simple question that might have been already located in the treatment plan we could look it up… if it's something that really is medically specific we'll send it over to the doctor and then reply on behalf of the practitioner." — *Ep 74* (triage logic for the agent: answer simple, escalate clinical)
- `[V]` "we hired three virtual assistants. I did one webinar to my colleagues and we launched it… we're only accepting five clients and we had this massive wait list already." — *Ep 69* (demand proof)

### NO-SHOW / LATE-CANCEL — language & policy (Ep 79 is the motherlode)
- `[V]` "how we deal with clients that overbook, late cancel or no show" — the exact framing of the pain. — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "a lot of things can be resolved with conversation… have a conversation with them after the first no-show or after the first late cancellation, just remind them of the policy." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "on our forms we get people to initial where it says this is a consequence for late cancellation and this is a consequence for no-show, because usually people tend to gloss over things… if there's a clause where they have to initial then they usually read that more carefully." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "there is a financial cost to missing a session… that's a spot that could have been filled by another client… everybody's time is valuable." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "the other thing that's important is that you actually uphold the policy. There's no point in putting a policy in place… and then someone showing up and then you're like oh let's just let it slide for this first second third fourth time, then people will not respect your time." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "we keep credit card on file… if they've missed their session and the card doesn't work we're going to be following up with a phone call. If they're avoiding phone calls we follow up with a letter." — *Ep 79* (the dunning ladder)
- `[V]` (admin script for missed payment) "we're so happy to have you on as a client. We noticed that you missed an appointment, we tried to run the card it was unsuccessful, wondering how you might be able to settle that." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "to view policies as fair for everyone and not punitive… we don't want to punish you not showing up." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "how do you draw the line and remove that feeling of guilt for following your own policies?" … "when my clients go to work they expect a full paycheck… all of the people on my team work really hard, they deserve to get paid, so I have to park that guilt." — *Ep 79* (money-guilt named directly)
- `[V]` (perpetual 49-hour canceller) "I think sometimes patients just don't understand how it disrupts things on the back end and a little communication can go a very long way." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "do they offer tele medicine for last minute changes or cancellations… instead of canceling them, that appointment type would just be changed to online" — pivot-to-virtual to save the slot. — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "some people have cancellation lists, so if someone needs an appointment sooner… and someone cancels they might be able to fit them in." — *Ep 79* (waitlist fill — natural voice-agent task)

### After-hours / safety
- `[V]` "where I live at 5 o'clock it's dark now so we actually lock the door after 5:00 and clients know that when they arrive they can just text us or call us… we grab the door and let them in." — *Ep 79* (after-hours contact behavior)

### Doctor vs front-desk boundary (who the agent represents)
- `[V]` "it's uncomfortable for a doctor to be in a situation where they need to reprimand… you don't want that, it messes with the relationship… really empower the front desk team to have what's necessary to handle a problem client, so that when you're in your sessions it's really about the relationship you're cultivating with the patient." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "the patient can then try to circumvent the admin staff by going to the provider saying 'I know I was late but can you make this allowance this one time'… it really is the responsibility of the admin team." — *Ep 79*

- `[V]` "if someone needs at least seven times to notice something, you've got to find other ways to do it." — *Ep 49* (frequency / 7-touches belief)

- `[V]` "don't just hide in your room and go on Instagram, but get out there and meet all the neighbors… we had a big grand opening party, we invited the mayor to do a ribbon cutting." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "the community had been asking like when are you going to have Physio and when are you going to have Chiro" — demand-driven service expansion. — *Ep 49*
- `[V]` "my target demographic… a female between the ages of 35 to 45 with a gross household income of $250,000 associated with one or more social groups… I found locations based on that market. I'm by a Trader Joe's, a hospital system, Whole Foods." — *Ep 64* (explicit ICP + at-capacity, niche-referral model)
- `[V]` "every chiropractor is going to offer something unique. I focus on head neck and neurological disorders… there's a lot of referrals I do by simply having a niche market." — *Ep 64*

## 4. Objections & values

- `[V]` "we are a human powered company, we are tech enabled, and that is our superpower… [investors asked] how are we going to replace this aspect with AI… my answer was always: we're not. We're always going to empower them with the tools." — *Ep 74* (the care-over-automation objection, stated by an AH founder to investors)
- `[V]` "we don't ever want to lose that human touch because it's so critical… we are human powered first and tech enabled second, and that's why we can deliver compassionate care." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "these conversations can become so [topical/political] and like such a political debate but it's just a really practical thing going on here… let's look for areas where we can better automate our jobs to make our jobs more enjoyable." — *Ep 74* (the pragmatic AI framing to use in copy)
- `[V]` "creating a bespoke experience for every single patient… it sounds so beautiful and lovely to make this concierge experience but the reality is things fall through the cracks when we try to do things on an individual level every time." — *Ep 74* (standardization > artisanal — supports a systematized agent)
- `[V]` "when we standardize… it not only creates a better experience for the practitioner but it also doesn't mean the patient has a worse experience — in fact they're going to have a way better experience. Think of Starbucks… you know exactly what you're going to get every time, even if it's automated, it feels good because you know what to expect." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "we went through a couple of marketers at the beginning that we butted heads with… they weren't the right fit." — *Ep 49* (agency / vendor distrust — fit matters more than capability)
- `[V]` "finding and vetting someone… going with your gut on 'this is a good match for me' versus 'I just need somebody.'" — *Ep 49*
- `[V]` "do I think we could do more marketing? I always do, but it's just finding the smart ones that make sense." — *Ep 49* (skeptical of marketing spend; wants ROI proof)
- `[V]` "can I get a report on the Google analytics… we had discussed this percentage of an increase and it's nowhere near that, like what's going on?" — *Ep 49* (wants accountability from marketing vendors)
- `[V]` (on ChatGPT) "the first time I used it and I was sold was I had to write an email that was not so nice… you have to give it a scale of 1 to 10… that's when I was like, oh my gosh, how specific you can be with it." — *Ep 4, Use ChatGPT to write emails* (tech-hesitancy → conversion through a concrete win)

## 5. Insurance / booking / patient-experience phrasing

- `[V]` "we're not billers and we don't claim to be… we'll do insurance pre-authorizations, so that's a huge lift for clinic owners." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "here in Canada we might do some direct billing for clinics." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` "most insurance companies don't cover a cost for the session that you have missed [no-show], so that will come out of pocket." — *Ep 79*
- `[V]` "we want to check in on whether their intake form has been completed, because if it's not in then that practitioner is going to have to sit there for 15 minutes in a paid visit and actually collect all that information." — *Ep 74* (intake-form chase = key booking workflow)
- `[V]` "you get a different answer if you're sitting there in the office under time pressure… versus when you just do it at home." — *Ep 74*
- `[V]` (low-touch vs high-touch) "low touch is something automated, like a reminder that their intake form hasn't been completed yet; high touch is maybe picking up the phone and asking how their pain is after the visit… we can't be bespoke and have that high touch on every communication or we'll all burn out." — *Ep 74* (defines exactly where automation belongs)
- `[V]` (patient experience, Ritz-Carlton/Apple model) "using first name when they enter — 'Denzel, nice to meet you, 11:15 you're right on time'… a fond farewell and an invitation to return." — *Ep 64*
- `[V]` "we don't bill on the spot… she'll get an invoice and pays out within three to five days. We built that level of trust with our clients." — *Ep 64* (deferred billing as a trust signal)
- `[V]` "think about the entire patient journey — from the moment they find you or your website online, from the time they book, they speak to your front desk staff, they get their intake form sent to them, their drive to the clinic, the time of payment, and the experience after, rebooking their return consult." — *Ep 74* (the canonical patient-journey map)
- `[V]` "they finally find a practitioner that they think they can trust and they just book a whole bunch of sessions in… that overbooking really speaks to how much people are struggling and how relieved they are to finally find someone who can help them." — *Ep 79* (patient emotional state at booking)

## 7. Voice-agent design implications (synthesis)

- `[E]` The wedge is validated three ways in this bundle: (1) clinics literally ask VAs "answer our phone" (Ep 74); (2) 3-day response lag is normalized and shameful (Ep 74); (3) staff turnover makes the human front desk unreliable and expensive (Ep 74). An always-on agent attacks all three.
- `[E]` Triage rule for the agent is given verbatim (Ep 74): answer simple/logistical, escalate clinical to the practitioner — never give medical answers.
- `[E]` Highest-value outbound jobs surfaced: no-show/late-cancel follow-up + card-on-file dunning (Ep 79), intake-form chase before paid visits (Ep 74), patient reactivation/recall (30–40% revenue lift, Ep 74), and cancellation-list waitlist fill (Ep 79).
- `[E]` Tone constraints: must read as "human-powered, compassionate, fair-not-punitive," not salesy or replacing-humans — the loudest values objection in the vertical (Ep 74 founder pitched investors on NOT replacing staff). Frame automation as "freeing the front desk from putting out fires," not cutting them.
- `[E]` Reusable scripts to lift directly: missed-payment opener (#22), no-show reminder framing (#17–20, #25), after-hours "text or call us to be let in" (#28), and the Ritz/Apple call-close "fond farewell + invitation to return" (#39).
