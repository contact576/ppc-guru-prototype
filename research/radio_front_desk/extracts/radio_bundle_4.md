## 2. FRONT DESK / PHONE / NO-SHOW / REBOOKING PAIN  *(highest priority — voice-agent wedge)*

> Note: this bundle is light on literal "missed call / after-hours" language, but rich on the **front-desk-as-revenue-engine** theme — the admin/reception layer directly drives rebooking, referrals, and patient experience. Strong supporting material for "why the front desk matters."

- **The front desk is the FIRST job to train and the gateway to everything.**
  - [V] "the top three to five responsibilities to focus on is **how to answer a phone call**. What do our clinicians actually do? Like what does a Cairo [chiro] do versus a physio?... how to book a patient in." — *Ep 38: From onboarding to training, the first 30 days*
  - [E] Reception's #1 trained skill = answering the phone and booking — i.e. exactly the task a voice agent covers when the desk is unmanned. — *Ep 38*

- **Admin staff KNOW why patients don't rebook — front desk holds the conversion intelligence.**
  - [V] "Admin staff typically know why someone is not rebooking and they want your patients to succeed too. So I'm always like, 'Oh, you befriend your admin.' Like you need them to like you if you want to fill up." — *Ep 43: Ali's story from clinic owner to CEO of Jane*
  - [V] "I had a massage therapist once... that was not rebooking. It just could not build his case load. And I went up to my admin staff... and they told me, 'He tells a story that makes them uncomfortable.'" — *Ep 43*
  - [V] "he stopped telling that story and he started getting rebookings... sometimes... your admin staff know more than you." — *Ep 43*

- **Referral intake runs through the OTHER clinic's front desk — make it frictionless.**
  - [V] "It's not really Dr. Emma who's sending the person over. She's telling **her front desk staff** send them to Cairo. Make it easy on that person and honor that person cuz they often get forgotten in the world of healthcare." — *Ep 18: the referral secret that fills schedules*
  - [V] "key referral relationships that will be faucets that never stop dripping patients out... they're always getting two or three or four patients every week referred over." — *Ep 18*
  - [V] "you can send me an email. You can call our office. Whatever's easiest for you... well the doctor wants us to fax things over and just smile and say right on we use fax as well." — *Ep 18* (fax-era friction is real; appears twice — also Ep 28 "unfortunately fax still these days")

- **Follow-up persistence is a known weak spot owners admit to.**
  - [V] "number two follow-ups... a lot of people are like in our old head 'oh should I follow up I already sent an email now should I send another one' I'm just like send it send it send it send it... everyone's super busy everyone's coming going." — *Ep 78: Fit Club NY*
  - [E] Owners want more follow-up but feel awkward/short on time — automated, non-pushy follow-up is a clear value prop. — *Ep 78*

- **Patient experience = presence, not click-clacking at a screen** (the desk/admin friction patients feel).
  - [V] "it just allows me to be more present... not be typing away click click clacking on my keyboard... people just feel like they have your full attention." — *Ep 28*

## 4. Objections & values (not salesy, care-over-money, tech hesitancy, agency distrust)

  - [V] "I want them to leave that session like wow... I got to work with Dr Kels today and that was an awesome experience." — *Ep 78*

  - [V] "if you just focus on pain you're going to miss the mark... people will literally live with pain for seven eight... 10 years." — *Ep 78*

- **Tech / AI hesitancy (directly relevant to selling an AI voice agent).**
  - [V] "just getting over the hurdle of not knowing is actually the hardest part about adopting technology... you have to do that mental math... I know it's going to be a bit hard... but life will be so much easier and so much more efficient." — *Ep 23: The Hardest Part of Technology Adoption Isn't the Tech*
  - [V] "there's a feeling sometimes of wanting to bury our heads in the sand because it feels overwhelming. It can feel threatening at times too." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "it's going to move forward with or without me and I'd rather be a part of it... than [be left behind]." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "I'm not an engineer. I'm not into this stuff generally... so it was quite daunting, but you just got to do it. Just do it and then you'll be glad you did." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "don't let the prompt be a barrier to trying this." — *Ep 28*
  - [E] Adoption objection pattern: overwhelm + fear of not-knowing, NOT a values objection. Wedge = "start small, low-risk, we make it easy." — *Ep 23 / Ep 28*

- **Trust / liability — "the human stays in the loop" (critical guardrail for a voice agent pitch).**

- **AI/automation reframed as time + creative space back (the upside language to mirror in copy).**
  - [V] "how can this help me pick up the pieces that feel often draining as a business owner and really just help me give that energy back to my clients." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "That used to take me 25 minutes after every initial appointment. That's all gone all of a sudden." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "these tools give my creative brain space to breathe... it's not so focused on so many of these administrative tasks." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "I see it as a thinking partner." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "think about the things that you do on a regular basis that are a slight bit painful and then see how AI could help you with that." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "tasks eat up a lot of our time and it's not where we want to be spending all of our time... a lot of the time the good stuff is the first thing to fall out of our schedule." — *Ep 28*

- **Transparency / consent with patients about AI (model for how the clinic will frame the voice agent to callers).**
  - [V] "I just say... hey I'm using this tool... it just allows me to be more present." — *Ep 28*
  - [V] "I haven't had anyone decline using it yet. Most people... 'oh that's really cool... you guys are on the leading edge.'" — *Ep 28*

## 7. Voice-agent script implications (synthesis)

- [E] **Lead with relief, not tech.** Owners self-identify as clinicians, not businesspeople; copy that says "you didn't go to grad school to answer phones / chase rebookings" lands (quotes 1–3). Avoid engineer/jargon framing — "don't let [the tech] be a barrier" (28).
- [E] **Position the agent as a draft/assistant with a human in the loop**, never a decision-maker — mirrors how clinicians already accept AI scribes (quotes 20–23). This neutralizes the liability/trust objection.
- [E] **Anchor the wedge on the front desk's proven revenue role**: it answers the phone, books, and holds rebooking intelligence (quotes 8–10). A missed/after-hours call = a missed booking and a missed referral handoff (11–13).
- [E] **Use the "present / not click-clacking" frame for caller experience** (15) and the time-back frame for the owner (29–31) — the same dual benefit (better patient experience + reclaimed owner time) clinicians already credit AI scribes for.
- [E] **Transparency model for callers**: clinics are comfortable disclosing AI use and find patients react positively ("that's really cool, leading edge") (33) — the voice agent should be disclosable, data-safe ("not used to train the model... deleted after x days"), consent-friendly.
- [E] **Paid-ads fatigue is a sales angle**: owners burned $20k on radio and dismiss Facebook ads as low-quality, slow-to-follow-up leads (17–18). "Stop paying to generate leads you then drop because no one answered/followed up" reframes the agent as protecting existing demand (word of mouth) rather than buying new.
